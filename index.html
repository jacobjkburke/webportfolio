About Me

Jacob Burke

University of Washington Student
B.S. in Informatics - Human Computer Interaction
Seattle, WA

Hi, my name is Jacob Burke and I am passionate and fascinated with the intersection between people, technology and design. Driven by these interests, I aim to research and design user-centered products to help people use technology and ultimately achieve more. My education and experience in Human-Computer Interaction and Design have given me the toolset to research, design, build, and evaluate interactive technologies for a wide range of people. I believe in the importance of curiosity and am always pushing forward to innovate.


I am currently completing my education and am pursuing a double degree and a minor at the University of Washington; the first degree is a Bachelor’s of Science in Informatics: Human-Computer Interactions, the second degree is a Bachelor’s of Science in Informatics: Cyber Security, and the minor is in Geography: Geographic Information Systems. Listed below is my portfolio filled with course assignments and personal projects that demonstrate the skillset I attained through my education and of which I am proud of.


Outside of school and working on personal projects, I use my limited free time to produce, direct, and edit my own short films. I am always open to new challenges and learning new things, so if there is something you think I would be interested in, let’s get in touch.

Skills

User Experience

When designing, I am constantly thinking about the different ways the user will experience and interact with the product. I strive to incorporate user research and user values to all steps of the design process.

Strengths: User Research, Problem Solving, Natural Curiosity, Divergent & Convergent Thinking

Ideation

I love the entire process from ideation to prototyping and everything in between. During each step of the process, I enjoy externalizing ideas whether it be through a low fidelity prototype like a sketch or a high fidelity prototype like a wireframe. By doing this, I can express details, often revealing which parts of an idea are still ill- or undefined.

Strengths: Prototyping, Usability, Sketching, Wireframing, Illustrator, Premiere Pro

Value Proposition

I am confident in applying concepts from numerous flavors of design paradigms, but one I am particularly fond of is Value-Sensitive Design. Looking beyond just problems, tasks, and activities, I also explore values that people try to express in their lives. Through value proposition framing, my goal is to identify people who may not have been designed for otherwise.

Strengths: User empathy, User Research, Participatory Design, GenderMag

Critique

I understand the importance to both give and receive critique. I am always eager to learn from my failures because behind every bad idea is a reason for its failure that should be analyzed and integrated into understanding the problem. No one has enough perspective or information to know everything good and bad about design alone. Seeking the perspective of others on an idea helps me complete this picture.

Strengths: Growth Mindset, Accepting of Dissent, Design Testing, Design Emotional Distance

Collaboration

I prefer working with a team because I know, when executed properly with correct communication and collaboration skills, the final product always benefits. Successfully working with different partners between course work and personal projects, I know the strength of collaboration first hand. Additionally, I can understand and easily adjust to individual styles to best fit target audiences and project stakeholders.

Strengths: Planning, Leadership, Organized, Development Time Management

Coding

Coding is the strongest skill I have to turn my theories and designs into reality. Having experience with multiple coding languages gives me the essential tools to code, but more importantly, helps me grasp the challenges engineers face when presented with a design specification.

Strengths: Algorithms, System Architecture, End User Experience
Languages: Java, HTML, CSS, CSS Libraries, JavaScript
Exposure to: Git, React, Firebase, PHP, SQL, R, JSON, XML

Fifteen Slide Puzzle

This websites implants the famous puzzle known as The Fifteen Puzzle. It is a simple classic game consisting of a 4x4 grid of numbered squares with one square missing. The object of the game is to arrange the tiles into numerical order by repeatedly sliding a square that neighbors the missing square into its empty space.

Speed Reader

Everyone wishes they could read faster. One method for doing so is called Rapid Serial Visual Presentation (RSVP). RSVP programs lets users read faster by presenting words to the reader in quick succession. Therefore, the reader is only able to focus on a single word at a time. This website I created does just that.
